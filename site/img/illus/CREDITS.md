# Species Illustration Credits (Billfish, Tuna, Pelagic group)

Public-domain and free-license scientific fish illustrations (classic natural-history
plates and FAO/FishBase species illustrations), sourced from Wikimedia Commons.
Each source was downloaded, then processed with ImageMagick to remove the background
(transparent alpha) and trim. All outputs are PNG with an alpha channel.

## Reef and Bottomfish

scientific plates, processed to a transparent background to match the app's dark navy
theme. The groupers and rockfishes are tinted FMIB / Jordan and Evermann plates (olive,
tan, red); the snapper/pargo group and the giant sea bass use genuine Denton color
chromolithographs as same-genus or same-family stands-in. Where no color illustration of
a species (or close congener) exists, the grayscale plate was removed and the card falls
back to a color photo (noted below). Sources are public domain except where a Creative
Commons license is noted (attribution preserved here).

## Inshore and Surf

COLOR field-guide plates: classic chromolithographs and color scientific illustrations,
on plain, aged-paper, or already-transparent backgrounds, processed to a transparent
background to match the app's dark navy theme (silvery-bodied fish keyed via a smoothed
luminance mask so the pale body is preserved). Where no color plate of the exact Baja
species exists, a faithful same-genus (or same-family) congener plate is used as a visual
stand-in; this is noted per entry. Sources are public domain except where a Creative
Commons license is noted (attribution preserved here).

- `roosterfish.png` (Roosterfish, Nematistius pectoralis) [PHOTO FALLBACK, no illus PNG] [notFound]
  - Status: Re-verified, still notFound. No public-domain or Creative Commons COLOR illustration
    of the roosterfish exists. The species is monotypic (no congener to substitute), and every
    historical plate is monochrome: Jordan and Evermann 1900 Plate CXXXVIII (grayscale engraving),
    the Salviani 1554 "Papagallo" copperplate, and FMIB "Sea Angling in Mexico" are all grayscale;
    NOAA has no roosterfish illustration; the only color renderings are proprietary (Swainston) or
    photos. Per the color-only rule no PNG was created; the card keeps its COLOR photo at
    `/site/img/species/roosterfish.jpg`.

- `calico-bass`, `sand-bass`, `spotted-bay-bass` (Paralabrax clathratus / nebulifer /
  maculatofasciatus) [PHOTO FALLBACK, no illus PNG] [notFound]
  - Status: notFound. No public-domain or Creative Commons COLOR illustration of any of the three
    Eastern Pacific Paralabrax sea basses exists. The only historical plates are grayscale (Jordan
    and Evermann "Fishes of North and Middle America" Plate CXC, and the H. L. Todd FMIB "West
    Indian Cabrilla" engraving of P. clathratus). The Denton kelp-bass color plate from the 1913
    "Game Birds and Fishes" portfolio exists but is not available as a free PD/CC scan (only
    commercial reproductions). The available same-family Serranidae color chromolithographs (the
    Rock Hind grouper and Denton black sea bass) are already used for the cabrilla / golden-grouper
    and giant-sea-bass keys; reusing one across three more bass markers would be an obvious
    duplicate and would misrepresent a streamlined kelp/sand bass with a chunky grouper body. Per
    the "do not fake, do not use a photo" rule these three keys remain on their color-photo
    fallbacks at `/site/img/species/<key>.jpg`.

## Processing notes

- White / cream / aged-paper backgrounds were keyed to transparency. For silvery-bodied
  fish on light paper (corvina, white seabass, pompano, halibut, barracuda) a smoothed
  luminance alpha mask (median + blur, edge floodfill, morphological close) was used so the
  pale body is preserved instead of being eaten away; interior holes in the silver flank
  were sealed and any paper trapped between fins was floodfilled out by hand.
- Clean illustrations already on white / transparent backgrounds (snook, jack-crevalle)
  were edge-floodfilled to transparency to keep the pale body intact.
- FAO/FishBase illustrations have a solid navy-blue background, keyed out by color.
- The NOAA swordfish had a solid black background, keyed out by color.
- Caption text bands and frame borders were cropped before keying.
- Most sources are public domain. The snook (CC BY 4.0, Clara Costa D'Elia) and
  jack-crevalle (CC BY-SA 3.0, Kare Kare) are Creative Commons; attribution is preserved
  in their entries above.
- The Inshore and Surf group is now COLOR (chromolithographs and color scientific plates),
  not grayscale engravings. Roosterfish has no public-domain or CC color illustration, so
  its illustration PNG was deleted and the card falls back to a color photo.
- All outputs verified: PNG with alpha channel, sRGB (not grayscale), resized to fit
  within 1100x1100.
